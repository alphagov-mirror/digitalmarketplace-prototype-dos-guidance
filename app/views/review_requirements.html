Check before publishing

  • Have you specified the correct level of security clearance (if applicable) and stated that you would be willing to sponsor the supplier?
  • Is the latest start date reasonable given your procurement timeline and planning for any contingencies?
  • Does the expected contract length comply with the optional maximum 25% allowed? (eg you cannot state initial contract for 12 months with an option to extend by a further 12 months)
  • Do you have clearly defined essential criteria; relevant to your user and technical needs, and organisational goals and objectives; on which to conduct effective shortlisting?
  • Check that written criteria do not discriminate against suppliers. (it can be as simple as adding ‘or equivalent’ to criteria and setting clear scoring guidance for evaluators)

Once you have published your requirements you have limited scope to change any of the details. You can only edit your requirements if the new content would not have affected a supplier’s original decision to apply.
